A Hyperspectral Study into the Tar Balls Washing Up on Corpus Christi Beaches

Abstract: This study uses hyperspectral imagery and image classification to track and understand the tar balls washing up on Corpus Christi beaches. These tar balls continually wash up on the beaches of Corpus Christi, Port Aransas, and Mustang Island. Most of the tar stems from oil that leaked from the Ixtoc oil well which leaked 126 million gallons of oil into the Gulf of Mexico on March 23, 1980. A special combination of hyperspectral bands are used to identify the tar balls and oil slicks from the surrounding environment. A Maximum Likelihood Image Classification algorithm is then used to classify the tar balls.
